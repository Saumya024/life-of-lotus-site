Iyengar Yoga

Yoga, Breath & Embodied Disciplines

How It Works

  • Poses are held longer with precise attention to alignment
  • Props like blocks, straps, and bolsters help achieve correct positioning
  • Therapeutic applications address specific physical conditions
  • Teachers give detailed verbal instruction and hands-on adjustments

What People Typically Experience

  • Improved posture and body mechanics
  • Relief from chronic pain and structural issues
  • Deep understanding of how their body works
  • Increased body awareness and proprioception
  • Subtle opening that continues off the mat

Who This Is For

  • People with injuries or physical limitations who need adaptive practice
  • Those interested in the mechanics and anatomy of poses
  • Anyone seeking therapeutic benefits alongside general fitness
  • People who appreciate precision and detailed instruction

Frequently Asked Questions

Why so many props?
Props help you achieve proper alignment even if flexibility or strength is limited. They're tools for access, not crutches.
Is Iyengar good for back pain?
Often yes. The precision and therapeutic focus address structural issues. Many people with chronic pain find significant relief.
Is this slower than other yoga?
Poses are held longer, allowing deeper work. It's not about going fast but about going deep.
Do I need experience to start?
Beginners are welcome. In fact, the precise instruction makes it excellent for learning foundations.
Will I break a sweat?
It can be surprisingly challenging. Holding poses with precision requires sustained effort even without flowing sequences.
How is this different from generic yoga classes?
Iyengar teachers undergo extensive training (often 7+ years). The depth of knowledge and attention to detail is exceptional.
