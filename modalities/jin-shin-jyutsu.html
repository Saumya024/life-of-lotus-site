Jin Shin Jyutsu

Energy & Subtle-Body Practices

How It Works

  • Jin Shin Jyutsu is a gentle energy work practice that uses light touch on specific points
  • The practitioner places hands on "safety energy locks" along energy pathways in the body
  • These points correspond to different organs, emotions, and life functions
  • The touch is very light—just enough to connect with the energy flow
  • You remain fully clothed and lie comfortably while the practitioner works
  • The practice helps restore balance and flow in the body's energy system

What People Typically Experience

  • Deep relaxation and stress relief
  • Release of physical tension and discomfort
  • Improved energy flow and vitality
  • Emotional balance and calm
  • Better sleep and digestion
  • Reduced pain and inflammation
  • A sense of overall well-being and harmony

Who This Is For

  • People wanting gentle, non-invasive energy work
  • Those with chronic pain, fatigue, or stress
  • Anyone sensitive to touch who prefers light contact
  • People recovering from illness or surgery
  • Those interested in Japanese energy healing traditions
  • Anyone seeking deep relaxation and balance

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Jin Shin Jyutsu different from Reiki?
Jin Shin Jyutsu works with specific energy points and pathways in a particular sequence. Reiki uses hand positions and universal life force energy. Both are gentle, but the approach differs.
Is Jin Shin Jyutsu painful?
No. The touch is very light—just enough to connect with energy. It's one of the gentlest forms of bodywork.
How long is a typical session?
Sessions typically last 60-90 minutes. The practitioner works through a sequence of points, spending time on each as needed.
Can I learn to do Jin Shin Jyutsu on myself?
Yes. There are self-help techniques you can learn. Many practitioners teach self-care methods alongside receiving sessions.
Is Jin Shin Jyutsu safe during pregnancy?
Generally yes, as it's very gentle. However, inform your practitioner if you're pregnant, and consult your healthcare provider if you have concerns.
What should I wear for a session?
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothes. You remain fully clothed throughout the session.
