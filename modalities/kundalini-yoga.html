Kundalini Yoga

Yoga, Breath & Embodied Disciplines

How It Works

  • Combines movement, breath, mantras (repeated sounds), and meditation
  • Specific sequences called kriyas are designed for particular effects
  • Practices aim to move energy through the spine and energy centers
  • Sessions often include chanting and focused breathing techniques

What People Typically Experience

  • Intense energy sensations—tingling, warmth, or rushes up the spine
  • Emotional releases and shifts in awareness
  • Heightened intuition and clarity
  • Changes in sleep patterns, dreams, and energy levels
  • A sense of awakening or expanded consciousness

Who This Is For

  • People interested in the energetic and spiritual dimensions of yoga
  • Those seeking transformation beyond physical fitness
  • Anyone curious about working with subtle energy
  • People drawn to mantra, chanting, and sound

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Kundalini safe?
When taught properly with gradual progression, yes. Problems arise from forcing energy or practicing too intensely too soon. Find a qualified teacher.
What's the white clothing about?
Traditional Kundalini practitioners wear white to expand their aura and maintain elevated energy. It's not required for practice.
Will I have dramatic experiences?
Some people do; many don't. Subtle shifts are as valid as dramatic ones. Don't chase experiences.
Do I need to believe in chakras or energy?
Not necessarily. The practices have physical effects regardless of your beliefs. Experience first, conceptualize later.
How is this different from other yoga?
Kundalini includes more breath, mantra, and meditation than typical Western yoga classes. It's less about physical postures.
Is this connected to any religion?
It comes from Sikh and Tantric traditions but is taught secularly. People of all backgrounds practice it.
