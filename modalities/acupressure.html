Acupressure

Manual, Physical & Circulatory Therapies

How It Works

  • Pressure is applied to specific points on the body—the same points used in acupuncture
  • Finger pressure replaces needles to stimulate energy flow
  • Specific points are chosen based on your symptoms or goals
  • Pressure is held or rhythmically applied until energy shifts

What People Typically Experience

  • Relief from pain and tension
  • Reduced nausea and headaches
  • Improved energy and mental clarity
  • Better stress management
  • Sense of energy moving through the body

Who This Is For

  • People who want the benefits of acupuncture without needles
  • Those interested in self-treatment options
  • Anyone curious about energy-based healing
  • People looking for natural pain relief

Frequently Asked Questions

Is acupressure as effective as acupuncture?
For some conditions, they're comparable. Acupuncture may reach deeper; acupressure is non-invasive and can be self-applied.
Can I do acupressure on myself?
Yes, many points are accessible for self-treatment. Learning from a practitioner or reliable resources helps you locate points correctly.
Does acupressure hurt?
Some points are tender, especially when imbalanced. The pressure should be firm but within tolerance.
How quickly does acupressure work?
Some relief is immediate; deeper changes develop with regular practice over time.
What conditions respond to acupressure?
Commonly used for headaches, nausea, pain, stress, and fatigue. Effectiveness varies by condition and individual.
Are there risks to acupressure?
Generally very safe. Avoid pressing on open wounds, varicose veins, or during pregnancy (some points). When in doubt, consult a practitioner.
