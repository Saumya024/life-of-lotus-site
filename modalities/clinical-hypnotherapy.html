Clinical Hypnotherapy

Technology-Assisted & Complementary Practices

How It Works

  • A trained therapist guides you into a relaxed, focused state
  • In this state, the subconscious mind is more accessible
  • Suggestions and therapeutic work address specific issues
  • You remain aware and in control throughout

What People Typically Experience

  • Deep relaxation during sessions
  • Access to memories and patterns usually unconscious
  • Shifts in habits and behaviors that resisted change
  • Relief from anxiety, phobias, and other issues
  • A sense of uncovering and transforming deep patterns

Who This Is For

  • People wanting to change persistent habits
  • Those with anxiety, phobias, or trauma
  • Anyone seeking access to subconscious patterns
  • People who want drug-free approaches to psychological issues

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I lose control or do embarrassing things?
No. Clinical hypnotherapy isn't like stage hypnosis. You remain aware and won't do anything against your will.
Can everyone be hypnotized?
Most people can enter hypnotic states. Depth varies. If you can daydream or get absorbed in movies, you can likely be hypnotized.
Is hypnotherapy evidence-based?
Yes, for certain applications like anxiety, pain management, and habit change. Research supports its effectiveness.
How many sessions are needed?
Often fewer than talk therapy. Some issues resolve in 3-6 sessions. Complex patterns may need more.
Can I remember what happens?
Usually yes. You're not unconscious—just in a focused, relaxed state. You'll typically remember the session.
What's the difference between hypnotherapy and meditation?
Both involve altered states. Hypnotherapy is guided with therapeutic purpose. Meditation is typically self-directed practice.
