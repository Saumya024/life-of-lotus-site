How It Works

  • Your birth year, month, day, and hour are converted into four 'pillars' of heavenly stems and earthly branches
  • This creates a chart showing the balance of five elements (wood, fire, earth, metal, water) in your makeup
  • A practitioner analyzes which elements you have in abundance and which are lacking
  • Life cycles and annual luck pillars are examined to understand timing and phases

  • Better timing for major decisions like marriage, business, or career moves
  • Recognition of 10-year luck cycles and what each brings

Who This Is For

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between my Chinese zodiac animal and my full chart?
Your animal sign is just one piece. A complete chart includes four pillars (year, month, day, hour), each with its own animal and element. This creates a much more nuanced picture.
Why do Chinese astrology readings focus so much on elements?
Element balance is considered fundamental to health, success, and wellbeing. Understanding your elemental makeup helps identify what you need more of in your environment and activities.
What are luck pillars?
These are 10-year periods that influence the overall theme and opportunities of that phase. Knowing when your luck pillars shift helps with long-term planning.
Can Chinese astrology help with business decisions?
It's extensively used for timing business launches, hiring, and partnerships. Many business owners in Asia consult practitioners before major moves.
Is the Chinese zodiac year accurate if I was born in January or February?
The Chinese New Year falls between late January and mid-February. If you were born before the new year, you belong to the previous year's animal sign.
How does this connect to Feng Shui?
They're part of the same system. Your Chinese astrology chart can inform which Feng Shui adjustments would benefit you most based on your personal element needs.
