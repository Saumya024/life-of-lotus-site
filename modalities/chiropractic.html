Chiropractic

Manual, Physical & Circulatory Therapies

How It Works

  • A chiropractor assesses spinal alignment and joint function
  • Adjustments—quick, controlled movements—restore proper alignment
  • The focus is on the spine's relationship to the nervous system
  • Treatment may include exercises, ergonomic advice, and lifestyle recommendations

What People Typically Experience

  • Relief from back and neck pain
  • Reduced headaches
  • Improved mobility and function
  • A sense of alignment and ease
  • Sometimes unexpected improvements in other body systems

Who This Is For

  • People with back, neck, or joint pain
  • Those with recurring structural issues
  • Athletes wanting to maintain alignment
  • Anyone interested in spinal health

Frequently Asked Questions

Are chiropractic adjustments safe?
When performed by qualified practitioners, yes. Serious complications are rare. Inform your chiropractor of any conditions or concerns.
Why does my spine crack during adjustment?
The sound is gas releasing from joint fluid—similar to cracking knuckles. It's not bones cracking.
How often should I see a chiropractor?
For acute issues, more frequently at first. Many people maintain monthly or occasional visits for prevention.
Can chiropractic help with non-spinal issues?
Some conditions improve as spinal function affects the nervous system. Claims vary; research supports effectiveness for certain conditions.
What's the difference between chiropractic and osteopathy?
Both address structural issues. Chiropractic emphasizes spinal adjustments; osteopathy uses gentler techniques and emphasizes whole-body function.
Should I get imaging before chiropractic?
Not always necessary. Your chiropractor will recommend imaging if your case warrants it.
