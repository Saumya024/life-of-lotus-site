Kampo Medicine

Traditional Medical Systems

How It Works

  • Diagnosis includes pulse examination, abdominal palpation, and constitutional assessment
  • Standardized herbal formulas are matched to the patient's pattern
  • Formulas follow classical Chinese medicine principles adapted to Japanese practice
  • Many formulas are available in modern pharmaceutical-grade preparations

What People Typically Experience

  • Reliable, quality-controlled herbal formulas
  • Improvement in chronic conditions
  • Integration with conventional medical care
  • Fewer side effects than some medications
  • A systematic approach to selecting the right formula

Who This Is For

  • People in Japan or with access to Kampo practitioners
  • Those wanting standardized, quality-controlled herbal medicine
  • Anyone interested in Japanese adaptations of Chinese medicine
  • People seeking integration of traditional and modern medicine

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Kampo different from Chinese medicine?
Kampo evolved in Japan from Chinese medicine but developed its own diagnostic methods and formula selection. It's more standardized and integrated with modern medicine.
Are Kampo formulas regulated?
In Japan, many are approved pharmaceutical products covered by health insurance. Quality control is strict.
Can I get Kampo outside Japan?
Some formulas are available internationally. Access varies by country. Quality can vary, so source carefully.
Does Kampo have side effects?
Generally fewer than pharmaceuticals, but effects can occur. Proper diagnosis and formula selection minimize risks.
How long does treatment take?
Depends on the condition. Some acute issues resolve quickly; chronic conditions may need extended treatment.
Can Kampo be used with Western medicine?
Yes, it's commonly integrated in Japan. Inform all providers about what you're taking.
