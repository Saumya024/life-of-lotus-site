Traditional Medical Systems

How It Works

  • Diagnosis includes pulse reading, urine analysis, and detailed questioning about symptoms and lifestyle
  • Treatment combines herbal medicines, dietary guidance, lifestyle modifications, and accessory therapies
  • The system addresses root imbalances rather than just symptoms
  • Practice is traditionally integrated with Buddhist understanding of mind and body

  • A sense of the mind-body connection in their healing

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Sowa Rigpa different from Ayurveda?
Both have Indian roots, but Sowa Rigpa evolved in Tibet with Chinese and Central Asian influences. It has its own herbal formulas and is often practiced within Buddhist contexts.
Where can I find Sowa Rigpa practitioners?
They're most common in Tibetan communities and at Tibetan medical institutes. Some practice internationally in areas with Tibetan populations.
Are the herbs safe?
When prescribed by trained practitioners using quality ingredients, yes. As with any herbal system, quality control matters.
Does treatment involve Buddhist practices?
It varies by practitioner. Some integrate meditation and spiritual practices; others focus purely on medical treatment.
Can Sowa Rigpa treat serious illnesses?
It's used for various conditions traditionally. For serious illness, work with both traditional and modern medical providers.
How long does treatment typically take?
Like other traditional systems, it addresses patterns over time. Chronic conditions may require months of treatment.
