Steam Therapy

Hydro, Climate & Environmental Therapies

How It Works

  • You sit in a room filled with steam (moist heat at lower temperature than dry sauna)
  • Steam opens pores and respiratory passages
  • Sessions typically last 10-20 minutes
  • May be combined with essential oils or herbs

What People Typically Experience

  • Respiratory opening and clearing
  • Deep skin cleansing
  • Relaxation and stress reduction
  • Eased congestion from colds or allergies
  • Sense of purification

Who This Is For

  • People with respiratory issues
  • Those wanting skin cleansing
  • Anyone who prefers moist heat to dry sauna
  • People with sinus or congestion issues

Frequently Asked Questions

Is steam room or sauna better?
Depends on your goals. Steam is gentler, better for respiratory issues and skin hydration. Sauna provides more intense heat.
How often can I use steam rooms?
2-3 times weekly is typical. Some people use them more frequently without issues.
Can steam help with colds?
Many find it helpful for congestion and respiratory symptoms. It doesn't cure illness but may ease symptoms.
Is steam safe during pregnancy?
Generally advised against, especially in first trimester. Consult your healthcare provider.
Should I shower before or after?
Before (to be clean for shared facilities) and after (to rinse sweat and continue cooling).
Can I add essential oils?
Some facilities do. For home steam, you can add eucalyptus or other oils. Avoid if you have sensitivities.
