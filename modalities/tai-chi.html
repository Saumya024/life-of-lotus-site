Tai Chi

Yoga, Breath & Embodied Disciplines

How It Works

  • Tai Chi is a martial art practiced as slow, flowing sequences of movements called forms
  • Movements are performed with relaxed muscles, deep breathing, and focused attention
  • The practice emphasizes balance, coordination, and moving from your center
  • Forms can be short (5-10 minutes) or long (20-30 minutes), depending on the style
  • Regular practice develops internal strength, flexibility, and mental calm
  • It's often described as "meditation in motion" due to its meditative quality

What People Typically Experience

  • Improved balance and reduced risk of falls, especially in older adults
  • Greater flexibility and joint mobility
  • Reduced stress and anxiety
  • Better posture and body awareness
  • Increased strength, particularly in the legs and core
  • Enhanced mental clarity and focus
  • Better sleep and overall sense of well-being

Who This Is For

  • People of all ages, including seniors who want gentle exercise
  • Those recovering from injury or managing chronic conditions
  • Anyone seeking stress reduction through movement
  • People wanting to improve balance and prevent falls
  • Those interested in martial arts but prefer non-combative practice
  • Anyone looking for a meditative movement practice

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Tai Chi and Qi Gong?
Tai Chi is a specific sequence of movements (a form) that flows continuously. Qi Gong includes various exercises and can be practiced in different ways. Tai Chi is a type of Qi Gong, but not all Qi Gong is Tai Chi.
How long does it take to learn a Tai Chi form?
A short form might take a few months of regular practice to learn. Longer forms take longer. Mastery is a lifelong practice, but you can experience benefits while still learning.
Which style of Tai Chi should I learn?
Common styles include Yang, Chen, Wu, and Sun. Yang is most popular and accessible for beginners. The best style is often the one taught by a good teacher near you.
Can I learn Tai Chi from videos?
You can start with videos, but learning from a qualified teacher is recommended to ensure proper form and alignment. Many teachers offer online classes now.

How often should I practice?
