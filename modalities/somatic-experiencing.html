Somatic Experiencing

Somatic & Nervous-System-Based Practices

How It Works

  • A practitioner guides you to notice physical sensations connected to stress or trauma
  • You track where tension, numbness, or activation lives in your body
  • Gradual release happens through gentle awareness—not by re-living events
  • The process works with your nervous system's natural capacity to discharge trapped survival energy

What People Typically Experience

  • Nervous system settling that feels physical, not just mental
  • Release of long-held tension without having to talk it out
  • Better capacity to handle stress without shutting down or exploding
  • Feeling more present and grounded in daily life
  • Shifts in chronic symptoms that seemed unrelated to trauma

Who This Is For

  • People carrying unresolved trauma or chronic stress
  • Those who feel disconnected from their body
  • Anyone whose nervous system seems stuck in fight, flight, or freeze
  • People who haven't found relief through talk therapy alone

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to talk about what happened to me?
No. Somatic Experiencing works with body sensations rather than stories. You don't need to revisit or narrate traumatic events.
How is this different from regular therapy?
Traditional therapy often works primarily with thoughts and emotions. SE works directly with body sensations and nervous system activation.
How long does it take to see results?
Some people notice shifts after a few sessions. Complex trauma may require longer. Progress is often non-linear but noticeable.
Is this just for PTSD?
No. It helps with anxiety, chronic stress, and nervous system dysregulation from any source—not just major trauma.
Can I do this alongside other treatments?
Yes, it often complements psychotherapy, medical treatment, or bodywork. Inform your providers about concurrent treatments.
Will I feel worse before I feel better?
Some temporary activation can occur as the body processes. A skilled practitioner helps you stay within a manageable range.
