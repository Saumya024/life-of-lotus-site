Environmental Detoxification

Lifestyle, Preventive & Nature-Based Practices

How It Works

  • Assessment of your home and work environments for toxic exposures
  • Identification of problem sources—air quality, water, chemicals, mold, EMFs
  • Recommendations for reducing exposures through product changes and remediation
  • Support for the body's detoxification of accumulated toxins

What People Typically Experience

  • Improved symptoms when exposures are reduced
  • Better air and water quality at home
  • Awareness of previously unrecognized toxic sources
  • Overall improved health as body burden decreases
  • Peace of mind from a cleaner environment

Who This Is For

  • People with environmental sensitivities
  • Those with unexplained symptoms that might relate to environment
  • Anyone wanting to reduce toxic exposures
  • People concerned about chemicals in their environment

Frequently Asked Questions

What toxins should I be most concerned about?
Common concerns include mold, air quality, water contamination, cleaning and personal care product chemicals, pesticides, and building materials.
Is this different from internal detox?
Related but distinct. Environmental detox addresses external sources. Internal detox supports the body's elimination of toxins already absorbed.
How do I know if my environment is affecting my health?
Clues include symptoms improving away from home, sensitivity to chemicals, or symptoms that began after a move or renovation.
Is mold really a big problem?
For sensitive individuals, yes. Mold produces toxins that can significantly affect health. Proper remediation is important.
What about EMF concerns?
Scientific consensus says low-level EMF is safe, but some individuals report sensitivity. Reducing exposure is a personal choice.
Where do I start with environmental clean-up?
Focus on where you spend most time—bedroom and home. Air and water quality are foundational. Then address products and materials.
