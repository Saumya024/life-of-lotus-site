  • Diagnosis is based on patterns of imbalance rather than disease names

  • The goal is restoring smooth flow of energy and addressing root causes

Frequently Asked Questions

What can TCM treat?
It's used for a wide range of conditions—pain, digestive issues, hormonal imbalances, emotional problems, and more. Research supports its use for various conditions.
Does acupuncture hurt?
Most people feel minimal discomfort. Needles are very fine and insertion is usually quick. Sensations vary from nothing to a dull ache.
Are Chinese herbs safe?
When prescribed by a qualified practitioner, yes. Quality and sourcing matter—work with practitioners who use tested, reputable suppliers.
How many sessions do I need?
Depends on the condition. Acute issues may resolve quickly; chronic conditions typically require a series of treatments.
Can I use TCM alongside Western medicine?
Generally yes. Inform all your providers about concurrent treatments to avoid conflicts, especially with herbs and medications.
What's the difference between TCM and Ayurveda?
Both are traditional systems using herbs, diet, and lifestyle. TCM emphasizes acupuncture and has its own energy model. They can complement each other.
