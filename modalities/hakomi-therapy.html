Hakomi Therapy

Somatic & Nervous-System-Based Practices

How It Works

  • Hakomi is a body-centered psychotherapy that combines mindfulness with gentle experiments
  • The therapist helps you notice present-moment body sensations, emotions, and beliefs
  • Through mindful awareness, you discover unconscious patterns that organize your experience
  • Small experiments—like suggesting a movement or phrase—reveal how your system responds
  • The process is non-violent and respectful, working with your defenses rather than against them
  • Healing happens as you become aware of and transform limiting core beliefs held in the body

What People Typically Experience

  • Greater awareness of how your body holds beliefs and patterns
  • Understanding of unconscious organizing principles that shape your life
  • Transformation of limiting beliefs through direct experience, not just insight
  • More choice in how you respond to situations
  • Integration of body, emotion, and thought in a more cohesive way
  • Healing of relational patterns through present-moment awareness

Who This Is For

  • People wanting to understand and change unconscious patterns
  • Those who find talk therapy helpful but want to work more with the body
  • Anyone interested in mindfulness-based approaches to healing
  • People dealing with relationship patterns, self-esteem, or identity issues
  • Those who want a gentle, non-invasive approach to deep work

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Hakomi different from other body-based therapies?
Hakomi uniquely combines mindfulness with gentle experiments to reveal unconscious organizing principles. It's non-invasive and works with your system's natural intelligence.
Do I need to have a meditation practice?
No. Hakomi teaches mindfulness in the moment as part of the therapy. You don't need prior meditation experience.
What are "experiments" in Hakomi?
Experiments are small, gentle interventions—like suggesting you notice a sensation or try a movement—that help reveal unconscious patterns in real time.
How long does Hakomi therapy typically take?
It varies. Some people see shifts in months; deeper work may take longer. The process is often efficient because it works directly with unconscious patterns.
Is Hakomi suitable for trauma work?
Yes, when practiced by a trauma-informed Hakomi therapist. The method is gentle and respectful of defenses, making it suitable for trauma when done skillfully.
What training do Hakomi therapists have?
Hakomi therapists complete comprehensive training through the Hakomi Institute or certified training programs. Look for certified Hakomi practitioners.
