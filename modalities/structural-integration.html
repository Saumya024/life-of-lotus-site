Structural Integration

Manual, Physical & Circulatory Therapies

How It Works

  • A practitioner works systematically through the body's fascial layers over a series of sessions
  • Each session addresses specific body segments in a designed sequence
  • The work reorganizes the body in relationship to gravity
  • The classic Rolfing series is 10 sessions, each with specific goals

What People Typically Experience

  • Significant changes in posture and alignment
  • Greater ease and efficiency in movement
  • Resolution of long-held tension patterns
  • A sense of being taller or more open
  • Fundamental shifts in how the body is organized

Who This Is For

  • People with chronic postural issues
  • Those seeking fundamental structural change, not just symptom relief
  • Athletes and performers wanting to optimize body use
  • Anyone interested in the relationship between structure and function

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this the same as Rolfing?
Rolfing is the original structural integration developed by Ida Rolf. Other approaches have evolved from it, using similar principles.
Does structural integration hurt?
It was historically known for being intense. Modern practitioners work within your comfort level. Communicate openly about pressure.
Why does it take 10 sessions?
The 10-series systematically addresses different body segments and their relationships. It's designed as a complete process, not spot treatment.
How long do results last?
Changes can be permanent, especially when supported by movement awareness. The body maintains its new organization.
Is this appropriate for everyone?
Most people can benefit. Those with certain health conditions should consult with practitioners about appropriateness.
Can I do just a few sessions?
Some practitioners offer shorter series or targeted work, though the full series is designed as an integrated process.
