Reiki

Energy & Subtle-Body Practices

How It Works

  • A practitioner places hands lightly on or above your body
  • Universal life energy is channeled to areas that need healing
  • Sessions are typically quiet and relaxing, fully clothed
  • Practitioners are attuned through training to channel this energy

What People Typically Experience

  • Deep relaxation, sometimes falling asleep during sessions
  • Warmth, tingling, or a sense of heaviness lifting
  • Emotional release without needing to discuss what's surfacing
  • Improved sleep and reduced anxiety afterward
  • A sense of peace and balance

Who This Is For

  • People seeking relaxation that goes deeper than massage
  • Those open to subtle energy work
  • Anyone recovering from illness, grief, or exhaustion
  • People wanting gentle, non-invasive healing

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to believe in Reiki for it to work?
Belief isn't required. Many skeptics report feeling effects. Stay open and see what you experience.
What does the energy feel like?
Common sensations include warmth, tingling, heaviness, or lightness. Some people feel nothing physical but notice emotional or mental shifts.
Can Reiki replace medical treatment?
Reiki is complementary, not alternative. It supports healing but doesn't replace medical care for serious conditions.
How many sessions do I need?
One session can help. A series (often 3-4) allows deeper work. Many people continue periodically for maintenance.
Can I learn to do Reiki on myself?
Yes. After training and attunement, you can practice self-Reiki daily.
Is Reiki connected to a religion?
It emerged from Japanese spiritual practices but is not a religion. People of all faiths practice Reiki.
