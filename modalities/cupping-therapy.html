Cupping Therapy

Manual, Physical & Circulatory Therapies

How It Works

  • Cups are placed on the skin and suction is created (through heat or pumps)
  • The suction draws blood to the area and lifts the underlying tissue
  • Cups may be left stationary or moved across the skin
  • Sessions typically leave circular marks that fade over days

What People Typically Experience

  • Relief from muscle tension and pain
  • Improved circulation in treated areas
  • Release of stagnation and congestion
  • Relaxation and stress relief
  • A distinctive pulling sensation during treatment

Who This Is For

  • People with chronic muscle tension or pain
  • Athletes seeking recovery support
  • Those interested in traditional Asian healing methods
  • Anyone whose condition hasn't responded to other therapies

Frequently Asked Questions

Do the marks from cupping indicate a problem?
In traditional practice, darker marks suggest more stagnation in that area. The marks are not bruises from injury.
How long do cupping marks last?
Usually a few days to a week, depending on how much stagnation was present.
Is cupping painful?
Most people find it a pleasant pulling sensation, not painful. Pressure can be adjusted.

Can anyone get cupping?
Most people can. Those with bleeding disorders, skin conditions, or who are pregnant should consult a practitioner first.
Does cupping have scientific support?
Some research supports it for pain and muscle conditions. The field is still developing.
