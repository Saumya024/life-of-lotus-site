Massage Therapy

Manual, Physical & Circulatory Therapies

How It Works

  • A therapist uses hands, fingers, elbows, or tools to manipulate soft tissues
  • Techniques range from gentle relaxation strokes to deep pressure work
  • Sessions may focus on specific problem areas or the whole body
  • Various styles exist: Swedish, deep tissue, Thai, sports, lymphatic, and more

What People Typically Experience

  • Release of muscle tension and chronic tightness
  • Reduced stress and improved relaxation
  • Better circulation and reduced swelling
  • Pain relief for various musculoskeletal issues
  • Improved sleep and overall sense of wellbeing

Who This Is For

  • People with chronic muscle tension or pain
  • Those seeking stress relief and relaxation
  • Athletes needing recovery support
  • Anyone wanting regular body maintenance

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I get massage?
For maintenance, monthly is common. For chronic issues, weekly or biweekly until improvement, then less frequently.
Will deep tissue massage hurt?
It can be intense but shouldn't be unbearable. Communicate with your therapist about pressure preferences.
What's the difference between massage types?
Swedish is relaxation-focused. Deep tissue addresses chronic tension. Thai combines stretching. Sports focuses on athletic needs. Each has its purpose.
Can massage help with anxiety?
Yes, it activates the relaxation response and reduces stress hormones. Many people find it helpful for anxiety management.
