Frequently Asked Questions

Is fasting safe?
For most healthy adults, yes. Those with diabetes, eating disorders, or certain conditions need medical guidance. Longer fasts require supervision.
Won't I be hungry the whole time?
Hunger comes in waves and often decreases after the first day or two. Your body adapts to the fasted state.
What about intermittent fasting?
Usually means eating within an 8-hour window, fasting 16 hours daily. This is gentler and more accessible than extended fasts.
Can I exercise while fasting?
Light activity is usually fine. Intense exercise may need modification. Listen to your body.
What can I consume during a fast?
Depends on the protocol. Many allow water, tea, and coffee. Some include bone broth or minimal calories.
Who shouldn't fast?
Those with eating disorders, pregnant or nursing women, underweight individuals, and people with certain medical conditions should avoid fasting or get medical guidance.
