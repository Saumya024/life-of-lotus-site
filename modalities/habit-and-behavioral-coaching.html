Habit & Behavioral Coaching

Lifestyle, Preventive & Nature-Based Practices

How It Works

  • A coach helps you identify habits you want to change or develop
  • Behavioral science principles inform the approach—cues, routines, rewards
  • Small, sustainable changes build into larger patterns over time
  • Accountability and support structures maintain motivation

What People Typically Experience

  • Success with changes that previously failed
  • Understanding of why habits were difficult to change
  • Sustainable new habits that stick
  • Increased self-efficacy and confidence
  • Progress toward health and life goals

Who This Is For

  • People who've struggled to change habits on their own
  • Those wanting evidence-based approaches to behavior change
  • Anyone seeking accountability and structure for changes
  • People working on health behaviors—exercise, nutrition, sleep, stress

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from regular coaching?
Habit coaching specifically uses behavioral science research. It's more focused on the mechanics of behavior change than general life coaching.
Why do habits fail?
Usually because they rely on willpower rather than systems. Good habit coaching addresses environment, cues, and rewards—not just motivation.
How long does it take to form a habit?
Research suggests 2-8 months depending on complexity—not the often-quoted 21 days. Patience and consistency matter.
Can coaching help with health behaviors?
Yes, it's particularly effective for behaviors like exercise, nutrition, sleep, and stress management.
Is this covered by insurance?
Rarely directly, though some health programs include coaching. Many people pay out-of-pocket.
What if I've failed at habit change before?
Previous failure often means the approach was wrong, not you. Good coaching identifies what didn't work and tries different strategies.
