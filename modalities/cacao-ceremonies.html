Cacao Ceremonies

Shamanic, Ritual & Ceremonial Practices

How It Works

  • Ceremonial-grade cacao (raw chocolate) is prepared as a warm drink
  • The cacao is consumed in a group ritual setting
  • Heart-opening effects support meditation, movement, or sharing
  • Facilitators guide the experience, often including music and intention-setting

What People Typically Experience

  • A warm, heart-opening sensation
  • Increased emotional access and expression
  • Connection with others in the ceremony
  • Gentle, non-hallucinogenic expansion
  • Creativity and clarity

Who This Is For

  • Those interested in heart-opening work
  • Anyone curious about ceremonial practices without psychedelics

Frequently Asked Questions

Is cacao psychoactive?
Mildly. Ceremonial doses contain theobromine and other compounds that affect mood. It's not hallucinogenic but can shift state.
Is cacao ceremony safe?
For most people, yes. Those on certain medications (MAOIs, antidepressants) should be cautious. High doses can be intense.
What happens in a ceremony?
Varies by facilitator. Typically includes intention-setting, drinking cacao, meditation or movement, music, and often sharing in circle.
How is ceremonial cacao different from regular chocolate?
Ceremonial grade is minimally processed, retaining more of the psychoactive compounds. Regular chocolate is heavily processed with sugar added.
Do I need any special preparation?
Light eating beforehand is recommended. Arriving with clear intention helps. Some ceremonies suggest avoiding certain substances.
How often can I do cacao ceremonies?
Weekly is common for regular practitioners. There's no strict limit for healthy individuals.
