Sensorimotor Psychotherapy

Somatic & Nervous-System-Based Practices

How It Works

  • Combines talk therapy with direct attention to body sensations and movement
  • The therapist helps you notice physical patterns connected to psychological issues
  • Stuck body responses from past experiences are safely completed or transformed
  • Mindfulness of bodily experience is central to the approach

What People Typically Experience

  • Understanding of how their body holds their history
  • Completion of defensive movements that were interrupted
  • Greater access to body wisdom and intuition
  • Relief from chronic physical tension connected to emotional issues
  • Integration of traumatic memories at body level

Who This Is For

  • People whose bodies seem to carry their emotional history
  • Those who find traditional talk therapy insufficient
  • Anyone noticing physical patterns connected to psychological issues
  • People ready for a slow, thorough approach to healing

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from regular psychotherapy?
It includes the body as a primary source of information and change, not just the mind. Physical sensations and movements are actively worked with.
Is this appropriate for severe trauma?
Yes, when practiced by well-trained therapists. The approach has specific methods for working safely with complex trauma.
How long are treatment courses typically?
It varies significantly based on individual needs. Some people work for months, others for years. It's not a quick-fix approach.
Do I need to exercise or move a lot?
Not necessarily. Movements are often small and subtle. It's about awareness and completing natural body responses, not exercise.
