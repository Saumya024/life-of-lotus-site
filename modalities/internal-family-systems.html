Internal Family Systems (IFS)

Psychology & Mind

How It Works

  • IFS views the mind as containing multiple "parts" or sub-personalities, each with its own perspective and role
  • You learn to identify and communicate with these parts—protective parts, wounded parts, and your core Self
  • The therapist helps you access your Self, which has qualities like curiosity, compassion, and calm
  • From this Self-led state, you can understand and heal parts that carry pain or use protective strategies
  • Parts are not eliminated but rather unburdened and allowed to take on healthier roles

What People Typically Experience

  • Greater self-compassion as you understand why parts behave as they do
  • Reduced inner conflict as parts feel heard and understood
  • Access to a calmer, more centered state (Self-energy)
  • Healing of old wounds without having to relive trauma in detail
  • More choice in how you respond to triggers, rather than automatic reactions
  • Integration of previously conflicting aspects of yourself

Who This Is For

  • People who feel divided or conflicted within themselves
  • Those struggling with self-criticism or inner voices
  • Anyone wanting to understand and heal their relationship with themselves
  • People dealing with trauma, anxiety, depression, or relationship patterns
  • Those who find traditional therapy helpful but want a more structured approach to inner work

Frequently Asked Questions

Is IFS the same as having multiple personalities?
No. IFS uses "parts" as a normal psychological concept—everyone has parts. It's not the same as Dissociative Identity Disorder, which is a clinical condition.
Do I need to believe in this parts model for it to work?
Not necessarily. Many people find it helpful as a practical framework for understanding their inner experience, regardless of whether they take it literally.
How long does IFS therapy typically take?
It varies. Some people see shifts in a few sessions; deeper work may take months or longer. The pace depends on your goals and what you're working with.
Can I practice IFS on my own?
Some people learn to work with parts independently, but starting with a trained IFS therapist is recommended to learn the model properly.
Is IFS evidence-based?
Research on IFS is growing. It's been studied for conditions like depression, trauma, and anxiety, with promising results. More research is ongoing.
What if I can't access my Self?
This is common, especially early on. A trained IFS therapist helps you identify what's blocking Self-access and works with those protective parts first.
