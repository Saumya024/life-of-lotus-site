Naturopathy

Traditional Medical Systems

How It Works

  • A practitioner assesses your whole health picture—diet, lifestyle, stress, environment
  • Treatment combines multiple modalities: nutrition, herbs, hydrotherapy, and lifestyle changes
  • The focus is on supporting the body's innate healing capacity
  • Prevention and education are emphasized alongside treatment

What People Typically Experience

  • Comprehensive approach addressing multiple factors at once
  • Empowerment through understanding their own health
  • Gradual improvement in chronic conditions
  • Fewer medications needed over time
  • A collaborative relationship with their practitioner

Who This Is For

  • People wanting natural approaches to health
  • Those with chronic conditions seeking root-cause treatment
  • Anyone interested in prevention and optimal wellness
  • People who want to reduce dependence on pharmaceuticals

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a naturopath and a homeopath?
Naturopaths use many modalities—nutrition, herbs, hydrotherapy, etc. Homeopaths specialize in homeopathic remedies specifically. Many naturopaths also use homeopathy.
Are naturopaths real doctors?
Naturopathic doctors (NDs) complete accredited programs and are licensed in many jurisdictions. Training and regulation vary by location.
Can naturopathy treat serious conditions?
It's often used alongside conventional care for serious conditions. For conditions requiring urgent medical intervention, conventional medicine is primary.
How often do I need appointments?
Initial visits are longer; follow-ups track progress. Frequency depends on your condition—monthly is common during active treatment.
Will I have to give up medications?
That's a decision made with your medical doctor. Many people reduce medications over time as their health improves.
Is naturopathy covered by insurance?
Coverage varies by location and plan. Check with your insurer. Many people pay out-of-pocket.
