Siddha Medicine

Traditional Medical Systems

How It Works

  • Diagnosis includes pulse reading, examination, and assessment of the patient's constitution
  • Treatment uses herbs, minerals, metals (specially processed), and dietary modifications
  • The system is based on elemental theory and the relationship between body and cosmos
  • Practices were developed by ancient Siddha sages in South India

What People Typically Experience

  • Strong, traditional remedies that have been used for thousands of years
  • Treatment addressing both physical and energetic dimensions
  • Unique formulations not found in other systems
  • Long-term improvement in chronic conditions
  • Connection to an ancient lineage of healing

Who This Is For

  • People with roots in Tamil and South Indian traditions
  • Those seeking ancient, powerful healing methods
  • Anyone interested in mineral and metal-based traditional medicines
  • People with chronic conditions unresponsive to other treatments

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Siddha different from Ayurveda?
Both are Indian systems, but Siddha developed independently in Tamil Nadu with its own philosophy, herbs, and techniques. Siddha uses more mineral and metal preparations.
Are metal-based medicines safe?
When prepared correctly by qualified practitioners, traditional metal formulations are considered safe. The purification processes are extensive and specific.
Where can I find Siddha practitioners?
Primarily in Tamil Nadu, India. Some practitioners work internationally, especially in areas with Tamil diaspora.
What conditions is Siddha known for treating?
It's traditionally used for skin conditions, chronic diseases, and rejuvenation. Specific formulations address various conditions.
How long does Siddha treatment take?
Like other traditional systems, it works gradually. Chronic conditions may require extended treatment periods.
Can I use Siddha with conventional medicine?
Consult both practitioners. Some Siddha formulations may interact with medications.
