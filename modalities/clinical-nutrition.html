Clinical & Functional Nutrition

Plant, Herbal & Nutritional Therapies

How It Works

  • A practitioner assesses your diet, health history, and specific conditions
  • Testing may include blood work, food sensitivity panels, or nutrient analysis
  • Personalized nutrition plans address deficiencies and therapeutic goals
  • Supplements may be recommended alongside dietary changes

What People Typically Experience

  • Improvement in symptoms through dietary modification
  • Understanding of which foods support or undermine their health
  • Correction of nutritional deficiencies
  • Clearer relationship between eating and feeling
  • Prevention and management of chronic conditions through diet

Who This Is For

  • People with chronic health conditions affected by nutrition
  • Those wanting personalized rather than generic dietary advice
  • Anyone interested in food as medicine
  • People with digestive issues, fatigue, or inflammation

Frequently Asked Questions

How is clinical nutrition different from general dietetics?
Clinical nutrition often involves more detailed assessment and may use therapeutic doses of nutrients. Focus is on treating conditions, not just meeting basic requirements.
Will I need expensive testing?
Not necessarily. Some practitioners work with detailed history; others use testing. Discuss what's appropriate for your situation and budget.
How long until I see results?
Depends on the condition. Some changes bring quick improvement; reversing nutritional deficiencies or chronic patterns takes longer.
Do I need to follow a strict diet?
Recommendations vary by condition. Some require strict protocols; others involve gradual adjustments.
Is this covered by insurance?
Varies by plan and practitioner credentials. Some nutritionists and dietitians are covered; others aren't.
What credentials should I look for?
Look for registered dietitians, certified nutrition specialists, or practitioners trained in clinical nutrition.
