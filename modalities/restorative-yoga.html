Restorative Yoga

Yoga, Breath & Embodied Disciplines

How It Works

  • Poses are supported by props—blankets, bolsters, blocks—so muscles can fully relax
  • Only 4-6 poses are typically practiced in an hour-long session
  • The body is in positions that allow the nervous system to shift into rest-and-digest mode
  • No muscular effort is required; the props do the work

What People Typically Experience

  • Deep relaxation that differs from sleep
  • Nervous system reset after chronic stress
  • Release of long-held tension without effort
  • Improved sleep and reduced anxiety
  • A sense of being held and supported

Who This Is For

  • People experiencing burnout, exhaustion, or chronic stress
  • Those recovering from illness or injury
  • Anyone whose nervous system is stuck in overdrive
  • People who need permission to do nothing and rest

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Restorative Yoga too easy?
It's not about challenge—it's about restoration. For overworked nervous systems, this is exactly what's needed. There's nothing 'lazy' about healing.
Will I fall asleep?
Maybe, and that's okay if you need sleep. With practice, you learn to hover in deep relaxation while staying aware.
How is this different from just lying down?
The specific supported positions activate particular releasing responses. Plus, the guided environment helps you actually stay present rather than thinking about your to-do list.
Can I do this at home?
Absolutely. A few props (blankets, pillows) are enough to start. Many resources show home setups.
How often should I practice?
Even once weekly makes a difference. During stressful periods, more frequently. It's hard to overdo true restoration.
Is this useful for athletes?
Very much. Recovery is when adaptation happens. Restorative yoga supports deep recovery that active stretching doesn't provide.
