Shamanic Practices

Shamanic, Ritual & Ceremonial Practices

How It Works

  • Shamanic journeying uses rhythmic drumming or rattling to induce a trance-like state for traveling to non-ordinary realms
  • Soul retrieval involves a practitioner journeying to recover lost parts of a person's essence or vitality
  • Spirit guides or power animals may be encountered during journeys
  • Information, healing, or recovered soul parts are brought back from these experiences

What People Typically Experience

  • Vivid inner experiences while remaining conscious
  • Encounters with symbolic beings or landscapes
  • Guidance on life questions or healing
  • In soul retrieval, a sense of wholeness or integration after lost parts are recovered
  • A sense of connection to something larger
  • Deep relaxation and sometimes profound insights

Who This Is For

  • People drawn to indigenous spiritual practices
  • Those seeking guidance through inner exploration
  • Anyone curious about non-ordinary states of consciousness
  • People wanting to connect with inner wisdom through imagery

Frequently Asked Questions

Is shamanic journeying safe?
For most people, yes. It's not recommended for those with psychotic disorders. Work with experienced practitioners if new to it.
Do I need to believe in spirits?
You can approach it psychologically—as accessing the unconscious through symbolic imagery—or spiritually. Experience it and form your own understanding.
What's the difference from guided meditation?
Journeying involves entering a specific trance state with the intention to travel to other realms. Guided meditation varies more widely.
Can I journey on my own?
Yes, with practice and drumming recordings. Learning from experienced practitioners first is helpful.
What are power animals?
In shamanic tradition, animal spirits that offer protection, guidance, and power. You may encounter them in journeys.
Is this appropriating indigenous culture?
This is an important consideration. Core shamanic practices are often taught as universal techniques. Approach with respect and avoid claiming specific indigenous traditions.
