Sauna Therapy

Hydro, Climate & Environmental Therapies

How It Works

  • You sit in a heated room, typically 150-195°F, causing profuse sweating
  • Sessions last 15-30 minutes, often followed by cooling periods
  • Traditional saunas use dry heat; infrared saunas use radiant heat at lower temperatures
  • Multiple rounds with cold exposure between is traditional in some cultures

What People Typically Experience

  • Profuse sweating and detoxification sensation
  • Deep relaxation and stress relief
  • Muscle relaxation and pain relief
  • Improved circulation and skin condition
  • Social connection in traditional settings

Who This Is For

  • People seeking heat-based detoxification
  • Those with muscle tension or pain
  • Anyone wanting stress relief and relaxation
  • People interested in longevity practices

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I sauna?
2-4 times weekly is common for regular users. Daily use is traditional in some cultures like Finland.
What's the difference between traditional and infrared saunas?
Traditional saunas heat the air around you. Infrared heats your body directly at lower ambient temperatures.
Is sauna safe with high blood pressure?
Research suggests regular sauna use may actually support cardiovascular health, but consult your doctor if you have conditions.
Should I sauna before or after workout?
After workout is more common—heat relaxes muscles and aids recovery. Some use brief heat before exercise.
How long should each session be?
15-20 minutes is typical. Listen to your body and leave if you feel unwell. Build tolerance gradually.
Can sauna help with detoxification?
Sweating eliminates some toxins. Claims vary about the extent. The overall health benefits are well-documented.
