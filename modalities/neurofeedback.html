Neurofeedback

Technology-Assisted & Complementary Practices

How It Works

  • Sensors on your scalp monitor your brainwave patterns in real-time
  • You receive feedback (visual or auditory) based on your brain activity
  • By responding to feedback, you learn to shift your brainwave patterns
  • Sessions train the brain to function more optimally over time

What People Typically Experience

  • Improved focus and concentration
  • Reduced anxiety and better emotional regulation
  • Better sleep quality
  • Enhanced cognitive performance
  • Shifts in symptoms that weren't responding to other treatments

Who This Is For

  • People with attention or focus issues
  • Those with anxiety, depression, or PTSD
  • Anyone wanting to optimize brain function
  • People interested in drug-free approaches for cognitive issues

Frequently Asked Questions

Is neurofeedback evidence-based?
Research supports it for certain conditions like ADHD. Evidence for other applications is developing.
How many sessions are needed?
Typically 20-40 sessions for lasting change. Some people notice shifts earlier.
Is it safe?
Yes, it's non-invasive and has few side effects. Temporary fatigue or headache can occur occasionally.
Can neurofeedback help with anxiety?
Many people find it helpful for anxiety. Training specific brainwave patterns associated with calm can be effective.
How long do effects last?
Changes are often lasting because the brain learns new patterns. Some people do occasional maintenance sessions.
Is home neurofeedback equipment effective?
Consumer devices are less powerful but can be useful for maintenance or general training. Clinical equipment is more precise.
