Art Therapy

Expressive & Creative Therapies

How It Works

  • You create art—drawing, painting, sculpting, or other media—as part of the therapeutic process
  • A trained art therapist guides the process and helps interpret what emerges
  • No artistic skill is required—it's about expression, not technique
  • The art becomes a bridge to unconscious material and emotions

What People Typically Experience

  • Access to feelings difficult to express verbally
  • A sense of release through creative expression
  • Insights emerging from their own artwork
  • Connection to childhood creativity and play
  • Processing of trauma or emotions through non-verbal channels

Who This Is For

  • People who process better visually than verbally
  • Those with trauma or experiences difficult to talk about
  • Children and adolescents who may not engage in talk therapy
  • Anyone wanting creative approaches to psychological work

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be good at art?
Absolutely not. Art therapy isn't about skill or beauty. It's about expression and process.
What if I don't know what to draw?
The therapist provides prompts and guidance. Often the most meaningful work starts without knowing what will emerge.
Is art therapy just for children?
No, it's effective for all ages. Adults often find it accesses material that talking doesn't reach.
What happens to my artwork?
Practices vary. Some you keep; some remain with the therapist. Discuss preferences with your therapist.
Can art therapy help with trauma?
Yes, it's particularly useful for trauma. Non-verbal processing can be gentler and access different material than talk therapy.
How is this different from taking an art class?
Art therapy has therapeutic intent with a trained therapist. Art classes focus on skill development without the psychological processing.
