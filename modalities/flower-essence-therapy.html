Flower Essence Therapy

Plant, Herbal & Nutritional Therapies

How It Works

  • Preparations are made by infusing flowers in water, then preserved (typically with brandy)
  • Drops are taken orally, usually several times daily
  • Essences address emotional and spiritual states rather than physical symptoms
  • Selection is based on current emotional patterns needing support

What People Typically Experience

  • Subtle shifts in emotional patterns over days to weeks
  • Greater emotional balance and resilience
  • Awareness of emotional states that were unconscious
  • Support during transitions and challenges
  • Gentle, gradual inner changes

Who This Is For

  • People working on emotional or psychological patterns
  • Those seeking gentle support for personal growth
  • Anyone in emotional transition or crisis
  • People who find other approaches too intense

Frequently Asked Questions

How can flower preparations work if they're so dilute?
Like homeopathy, flower essences are believed to work through energy patterns rather than chemical content. The mechanism is debated but users report effects.
Are flower essences the same as essential oils?
No. Essential oils are concentrated plant compounds. Flower essences are energetic preparations, extremely dilute.
What are Bach Flower Remedies?
The original 38 essences developed by Dr. Edward Bach in the 1930s. Other systems have developed since.
Can I choose my own essences?
Yes, many people select based on descriptions matching their emotional state. Professional guidance can help with subtler issues.
Are there side effects?
Flower essences are generally considered safe. Some people experience temporary intensification of emotions as patterns shift.
How long until I notice change?
Some people feel shifts within days; others take weeks. Emotional changes are often subtle and gradual.
