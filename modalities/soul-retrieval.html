Soul Retrieval

Shamanic, Ritual & Ceremonial Practices

How It Works

  • A shamanic practitioner journeys on your behalf to find lost parts of your soul
  • According to this tradition, trauma causes parts of the soul to leave for protection
  • The practitioner locates and returns these parts
  • Integration work follows the retrieval session

What People Typically Experience

  • A sense of becoming more whole or complete
  • Recovery of vitality that had been missing
  • Return of memories, abilities, or qualities that had been lost
  • Emotional processing as parts reintegrate
  • Lasting shifts in how they feel and function

Who This Is For

  • People feeling incomplete or fragmented
  • Those who've experienced trauma or loss
  • Anyone sensing something vital has been missing since a specific event
  • People drawn to shamanic approaches to healing

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes soul loss?
Traditional understanding says trauma, shock, or prolonged stress causes parts of the soul to leave for protection. This includes accidents, abuse, surgery, or loss.
Is this compatible with therapy?
Many find it complementary. It addresses trauma from a different angle than talk therapy. Integration may benefit from therapeutic support.
Can I do soul retrieval for myself?
Advanced practitioners sometimes do. Generally, having someone journey on your behalf is traditional and often more effective.
How do I know if I need soul retrieval?
Signs include feeling not fully present, that part of you died with a loss, loss of joy or vitality after trauma, or gaps in memory.
What happens after retrieval?
Integration is important. You may need to welcome back and nurture the returned parts. Practitioners often give guidance for this.
Is soul retrieval a one-time thing?
Sometimes one session is sufficient. Others may benefit from multiple retrievals, especially with complex trauma history.
