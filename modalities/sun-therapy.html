Sun Therapy

Hydro, Climate & Environmental Therapies

How It Works

  • Controlled exposure to sunlight for therapeutic benefit
  • Duration is carefully managed to avoid burning—typically 10-30 minutes
  • Timing matters—morning sun differs from midday
  • Light therapy lamps can supplement or replace natural sunlight

What People Typically Experience

  • Improved mood and energy
  • Better sleep-wake cycles
  • Vitamin D production
  • Relief from Seasonal Affective Disorder
  • Sense of wellbeing and vitality

Who This Is For

  • People with Seasonal Affective Disorder
  • Those with vitamin D deficiency
  • Anyone lacking regular sun exposure
  • People wanting to regulate circadian rhythms

Frequently Asked Questions

How much sun exposure do I need?
10-30 minutes of midday sun, several times weekly, for most people. Skin type affects duration needs.
Can I get vitamin D through windows?
No, glass blocks UVB rays that produce vitamin D. You need direct exposure or supplements.
What about skin cancer risk?
Burning is the main risk. Brief, controlled exposure without burning is generally safe and beneficial. Don't overdo it.
Are light therapy lamps as good as real sun?
They help with mood and circadian rhythms but don't produce vitamin D. They're useful when natural sun isn't available.
When is the best time for sun exposure?
Morning sun helps set circadian rhythms. Midday sun produces most vitamin D. Evening sun is gentler.
How do I balance benefit and risk?
Know your skin type, avoid burning, get moderate regular exposure rather than occasional intense exposure.
