Tuina

Manual, Physical & Circulatory Therapies

How It Works

  • A practitioner uses various hand techniques—pushing, grasping, pressing, kneading—on acupuncture points and pathways
  • The work is typically more vigorous than other Asian bodywork
  • Techniques can be used alongside acupuncture or as standalone treatment
  • Sessions may include joint manipulation and stretching

What People Typically Experience

  • Release of muscle tension and stiffness
  • Improved circulation and energy flow
  • Pain relief for musculoskeletal issues
  • Greater mobility and flexibility
  • Overall sense of vitality

Who This Is For

  • People comfortable with vigorous bodywork
  • Those interested in Chinese medical approaches
  • Anyone with musculoskeletal pain or stiffness
  • People who want therapeutic rather than relaxation focus

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Tuina different from Shiatsu?
Both work with energy pathways. Tuina is typically more vigorous and may include manipulation. Shiatsu is often gentler with more sustained pressure.
Is Tuina painful?
It can be intense—more like therapeutic deep tissue than relaxation massage. Benefits often come from working at the edge of comfort.
Do I need acupuncture with Tuina?
Not necessarily. Tuina can be a complete treatment on its own, though it's often combined with other Chinese medical approaches.
What conditions does Tuina help?
It's commonly used for neck and back pain, headaches, sports injuries, and general stiffness.
How long are sessions?
Typically 30-60 minutes, depending on what's being addressed.
Is Tuina appropriate for everyone?
Most people can benefit. Those with certain conditions (fractures, skin conditions, pregnancy) should consult their practitioner.
