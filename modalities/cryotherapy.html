Cryotherapy

Hydro, Climate & Environmental Therapies

How It Works

  • The body is exposed to extreme cold, typically in a chamber reaching -200°F or below
  • Sessions are brief—typically 2-4 minutes
  • Whole-body cryotherapy involves the body while the head stays above the chamber
  • Local cryotherapy can target specific areas

What People Typically Experience

  • Intense cold followed by invigoration
  • Reduced inflammation and pain
  • Energy boost and mood elevation
  • Improved recovery from exercise
  • A rush from surviving the extreme cold

Who This Is For

  • Athletes seeking recovery enhancement
  • People with chronic inflammation or pain
  • Those curious about extreme cold therapy
  • Anyone seeking a powerful reset experience

Frequently Asked Questions

Is cryotherapy safe?
When done properly with trained operators, yes for most healthy adults. Those with circulatory conditions, heart disease, or cold sensitivities should consult doctors.
How cold is it really?
Extremely cold—much colder than any natural environment. The brief exposure makes it tolerable.
How often should I do cryotherapy?
Athletes often go 2-3 times weekly. Others may use it occasionally for recovery or invigoration.
Is this the same as ice baths?
Similar concept, different method. Cryotherapy is colder but briefer. Ice baths are more accessible but require longer exposure.
Does cryotherapy help with weight loss?
Some claim metabolic benefits. The evidence is limited. It's not a primary weight loss method.
What should I wear?
Protective gear for extremities (gloves, socks, ear coverage) is provided. You'll wear minimal clothing to maximize exposure.
