Frequently Asked Questions

How can such light touch do anything?
The touch works with subtle rhythms and tensions in the craniosacral system. Light touch can affect deep structures that resist stronger pressure.
Is this evidence-based?
Research is limited but some studies show benefits for certain conditions. Many people report significant subjective improvements.
What conditions does craniosacral therapy help?
Commonly used for headaches, TMJ, chronic pain, stress, and trauma. Benefits vary by individual.
How many sessions are needed?
Some people feel shifts after one session. Chronic conditions typically benefit from a series of treatments.
Can children receive craniosacral therapy?
Yes, it's often used with infants and children for birth trauma, colic, and developmental issues.
Is this the same as cranial osteopathy?
Related but distinct. Cranial osteopathy developed within osteopathy; craniosacral therapy emerged as a separate approach, often used by massage therapists and others.
