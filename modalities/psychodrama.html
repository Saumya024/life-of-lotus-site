Psychodrama

Expressive & Creative Therapies

How It Works

  • You act out scenes from your life with guidance from a trained director
  • Other group members or auxiliary objects play roles from your story
  • The action allows exploration of situations, relationships, and emotions
  • Processing follows the enactment, integrating insights with the group

What People Typically Experience

  • Relief from replaying difficult scenes and trying new responses
  • Insight from seeing their patterns enacted
  • Emotional release through dramatic expression
  • Understanding of others' perspectives by witnessing or playing roles
  • Breakthrough moments as stuck patterns become visible

Who This Is For

  • People who learn through action rather than discussion
  • Those with relational patterns they want to understand and change
  • Anyone drawn to drama and embodied approaches
  • People ready for group-based experiential work

Frequently Asked Questions

Is psychodrama the same as drama therapy?
Related but distinct. Psychodrama has specific techniques developed by J.L. Moreno. Drama therapy uses theatrical approaches more broadly.
Will I be forced to act?
Participation is always voluntary. You can observe or take smaller roles until comfortable.
Is this appropriate for trauma?
Yes, with skilled practitioners. Psychodrama can process trauma through action in a controlled, supportive environment.
How big are the groups?
Typically 8-15 people. Larger groups are possible with experienced directors.
What if I'm shy?
Many shy people find psychodrama helpful. The structure supports participation, and you can start with smaller roles.
How often should I participate?
Weekly groups are common. Some people attend workshops periodically. Frequency depends on your goals.
