Dreamwork

Shamanic, Ritual & Ceremonial Practices

How It Works

  • Dreams are recorded immediately upon waking
  • Various methods are used to interpret and work with dream content
  • Techniques may include association, amplification, dialogue with dream figures, or re-entering dreams
  • Dreams are seen as messages from the unconscious or as portals for healing

What People Typically Experience

  • Increased dream recall and vividness
  • Insights into unconscious patterns and feelings
  • Guidance on life situations from dream wisdom
  • Integration of disowned parts of themselves
  • A richer inner life and self-understanding

Who This Is For

  • People curious about their dream life
  • Those seeking self-understanding through inner exploration
  • Anyone wanting to work with nightmares or recurring dreams
  • People interested in the unconscious mind

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I remember dreams better?
Keep a journal by your bed. Stay still upon waking and reach for memories before moving. Set intention to remember before sleep.
What do dreams mean?
Interpretations vary by approach. Generally, dreams reflect unconscious material—personal, archetypal, or spiritual—in symbolic form.
Can dreams predict the future?
Some traditions say yes. Scientifically, dreams likely process information, though meaningful coincidences (and premonitions) are reported.
What about recurring dreams?
They usually point to unresolved issues seeking attention. Working with them often reduces or transforms their recurrence.
Should I use dream dictionaries?
They can provide starting points, but personal associations are more important. What does the symbol mean to you?
Can I control my dreams?
Lucid dreaming techniques can develop awareness and some control. This can be used for healing, creativity, or exploration.
