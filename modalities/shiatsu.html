Shiatsu

Manual, Physical & Circulatory Therapies

How It Works

  • A practitioner applies pressure with fingers, palms, elbows, or knees to specific points on the body
  • Work follows the same energy pathways used in acupuncture
  • Sessions typically occur on a mat on the floor, fully clothed
  • The pressure is held or rhythmically applied to promote energy flow

What People Typically Experience

  • Deep relaxation and stress relief
  • Release of chronic tension
  • Improved energy levels and vitality
  • Better digestion and sleep
  • A sense of energy flowing more freely

Who This Is For

  • People interested in energy-based bodywork
  • Those wanting relaxation without undressing
  • Anyone curious about Asian bodywork traditions
  • People with chronic fatigue or low energy

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Shiatsu like acupuncture?
Both work with the same energy pathways and points, but Shiatsu uses pressure instead of needles.
Do I need to undress?
No, Shiatsu is typically done fully clothed. Wear loose, comfortable clothing.
Is Shiatsu painful?
Pressure varies from gentle to firm. Some points may be tender, but it shouldn't be unbearable. Communicate with your practitioner.
How often should I receive Shiatsu?
For maintenance, monthly is common. More frequently for active issues.
What should I expect in my first session?
You'll lie on a mat while the practitioner works along energy pathways. Sessions are typically quiet and meditative.
Can Shiatsu help with anxiety?
Many people find it very calming. The nervous system relaxation can help with anxiety and stress.
