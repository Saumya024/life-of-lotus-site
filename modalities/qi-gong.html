Qi Gong

Yoga, Breath & Embodied Disciplines

How It Works

  • Qi Gong combines slow, flowing movements with breathwork and mental focus
  • Practices are designed to cultivate and circulate qi (life energy) through the body's energy pathways
  • Movements are typically gentle and can be adapted for any level of physical ability
  • Breathing patterns coordinate with movement to enhance energy flow
  • Meditative awareness helps you sense and direct energy within your body
  • Regular practice builds internal strength, flexibility, and vitality

What People Typically Experience

  • Increased energy and vitality over time
  • Greater physical flexibility and balance
  • Reduced stress and a calmer mind
  • Improved sleep and digestion
  • Better body awareness and coordination
  • A sense of internal strength and groundedness
  • Enhanced immune function and overall health

Who This Is For

  • People wanting gentle movement that supports health and energy
  • Those recovering from illness or injury who need low-impact exercise
  • Anyone interested in traditional Chinese energy practices
  • People seeking stress reduction through movement and breath
  • Those wanting to improve balance, flexibility, and body awareness
  • Anyone looking for a meditative movement practice

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Qi Gong and Tai Chi?
Qi Gong is more varied and can be practiced standing, sitting, or moving. Tai Chi is a specific sequence of movements (a form). Qi Gong is often simpler to learn initially.
Do I need to believe in qi for it to work?
No. Many people experience benefits simply from the movement, breathing, and meditative focus, regardless of how they conceptualize the energy aspect.
How often should I practice?
Regular practice is key—even 10-15 minutes daily is beneficial. Many people practice 20-30 minutes, several times per week.
Can I learn Qi Gong from videos or books?
You can start with videos, but learning from a qualified teacher helps ensure proper form and understanding of the principles. In-person or online classes are recommended.
Is Qi Gong safe for people with health conditions?
Generally yes, as movements are gentle and adaptable. However, consult with your healthcare provider if you have serious health conditions, and inform your teacher.
What styles of Qi Gong are there?
There are many styles—medical Qi Gong, martial Qi Gong, spiritual Qi Gong. Medical Qi Gong focuses on health and healing, which is often what people seek.
