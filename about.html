My Path to Astrology

I did not arrive at Vedic astrology as a subject of interest. I arrived through lived experience.

Loss, confusion, grief, and prolonged inner upheaval shaped important chapters of my life. During those phases, clarity disappeared, decisions felt heavy, and relationships tested both direction and identity. Strength did not return through sudden insight or emotional release. It returned slowly, through endurance, reflection, and the quiet rebuilding of self-trust.

Astrology entered my life during this terrain. Not as belief, comfort, or escape, but as a framework to understand why certain patterns repeat, why specific struggles arise when they do, and what aspects of life must be accepted versus consciously worked through.

Life of Lotus grew from this lived engagement with suffering, inquiry, and gradual clarity. It carries the imprint of experience, not abstraction.

What Is Vedic Astrology?

Vedic astrology, also known as Jyotish, is not a tool for reassurance or entertainment. It is a karmic map.

Rooted in the sidereal zodiac and ancient Sanskrit texts, Vedic astrology studies karma, time, and consequence with precision. It reveals:

  • long-standing karmic patterns embedded in the birth chart
  • unresolved tendencies carried across life phases
  • areas where effort is required versus areas where surrender is necessary
  • the nature of one's responsibilities, lessons, and limits

The birth chart reflects prārabdha karma, what is already in motion. It shows where awareness, discipline, and right action can soften or redirect experience. Some events unfold despite resistance. Others demand conscious participation. Wisdom lies in knowing the difference.

How I Practice Vedic Astrology

My work as a Vedic astrologer focuses on understanding:

  • how long it persists
  • what strengthens it

I use dashā systems, planetary periods, transits, and prashna, horary astrology, with restraint. Their purpose is not fear or false certainty, but orientation, so life can be navigated with steadiness, dignity, and responsibility.
